Balloon Breathing

This activity involves lying down and imagining a balloon in the tummy. As the child breathes in, the balloon inflates, and as they breathe out, it deflates. This helps children focus on their breath and relax.

Improved Emotional Regulation


Mindfulness helps children manage their emotions better, reducing stress and anxiety

while enhancing their ability to cope with challenges.

This leads to increased emotional resilience and better overall well-being.

Enhanced Focus and Academic Performance


Mindfulness practices improve focus and concentration, which are crucial for academic success.

By enhancing cognitive skills such as attention and memory,

mindfulness can lead to better academic performance and improved classroom behavior.

Better Social Skills and Empathy


Mindfulness encourages children to be more aware of themselves and others,

fostering empathy and compassion. This can lead to improved social interactions and relationships,

helping children navigate complex social situations more effectively.
